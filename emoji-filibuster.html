SENATOR FILIBUSTERS ENTIRELY IN EMOJI; 🫠 NOW LEGALLY BINDING

Congress Enters New Era of Legislative Pictionary, Chaos Ensues

WASHINGTON, D.C. — In an unprecedented moment of political theater and interpretive confusion, Senator Blake R. Spindle (I–NV) delivered a 14-hour filibuster composed entirely of emojis, turning the Senate floor into a real-time guessing game of vibes, feelings, and maybe policy(?).

The address, which began at 9:00 a.m. with “👋📜🗳️” and ended at 11:07 p.m. with “🌈🫠✨🇺🇸,” has now been entered into the Congressional Record verbatim, thanks to a loophole in the Language Modernization Act of 2024 (also written partially in emoji, allegedly by accident).

“I spoke from the heart,” Senator Spindle said afterward. “And also the thumbs. Mostly the thumbs.”

🫠 Emoji Now Legally Binding

In a shocking 5–4 decision, the Supreme Court ruled that “🫠” — the melting face emoji — now carries legal weight as an official expression of defeat, exhaustion, and “I cannot.” It may now be used to:

  • End legal arguments
  • Excuse missed votes
  • Replace a signature on emotionally draining documents

Justice Kagan’s concurring opinion, written entirely in lowercase, simply stated: “we’re all tired.”
