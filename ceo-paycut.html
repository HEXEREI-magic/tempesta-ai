CEO Takes “Voluntary” Pay Cut from $80M to $79.9M, Inspires Nation to Sacrifice

NEW YORK, NY — In a stirring act of selflessness, CEO Max Profitt announced a "voluntary" pay cut, reducing his annual compensation from $80 million to a mere $79.9 million. "We all have to tighten our belts," he said, before boarding his third helicopter of the day.

The move has inspired workers nationwide to consider similar sacrifices, such as switching to generic seltzer or only using one streaming service at a time.

Profitt’s board praised his "courage," then quietly voted themselves a bonus.
