Airlines Debut “Bring Your Own Seat” Fare—Standing Room Sold Separately

ATLANTA, GA — In a bid to maximize profits and minimize comfort, major airlines have introduced a new "Bring Your Own Seat" fare. Passengers are encouraged to bring folding chairs, beanbags, or "just stand and hold onto something."

"It’s all about choice," said an airline spokesperson. "You can pay extra for a seat, or you can pay extra to not have one. Either way, you’re paying extra."

Frequent flyers are already nostalgic for the days when legroom was measured in centimeters, not wishful thinking.
