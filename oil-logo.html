Oil Giant Unveils Eco-Friendly Logo; Climate Crisis Thanks Them for the PNG

HOUSTON, TX — In a bold step for the environment, oil conglomerate PetroMirth has unveiled a new green logo, printed on 100% recycled press releases. Executives say the logo’s leafy design will "absorb at least 0.0000001% of our annual emissions—on paper."

"We’re committed to sustainability, at least visually," said a spokesperson. The climate crisis responded with a polite email: "Thanks for the PNG. Please send actual change next time."

Shareholders applauded the move, then drove home in matching SUVs.
